Hikes

Greece is a paradise for hikers, offering a diverse landscape where ancient history, dramatic scenery, and rich local culture come together on every trail. From rugged mountain paths and winding coastal routes to volcanic islands and lush forested gorges, the country provides hiking experiences for every skill level. Whether you want to explore the legendary slopes of Mount Olympus, wander through the breathtaking Samaria Gorge, or follow sunlit cliffside paths on islands like Crete and Santorini, each hike reveals a new side of Greece’s natural beauty.

Along the way, you'll encounter traditional villages, archaeological sites, secluded beaches, and panoramic viewpoints that feel untouched by time. Many trails follow routes walked by ancient Greeks, offering a unique blend of outdoor adventure and cultural discovery. With its pleasant Mediterranean climate, crystal-clear waters, and landscapes that shift from mountains to sea in a matter of minutes, Greece delivers some of the most unforgettable hiking experiences in Europe.

Whether you're seeking a challenging trek or a leisurely nature walk, Greece’s trails invite you to slow down, explore, and connect with the country’s stunning environment in a deeper and more meaningful way.

Samaria Gorge in Crete

Carved into the rugged landscape of southwest Crete, the Samaria Gorge is one of Greece’s most legendary hiking routes—and a true bucket-list adventure for nature lovers. Stretching 16 kilometers through the heart of the White Mountains, this spectacular gorge leads you from the forested highlands of Omalos to the sparkling Libyan Sea, rewarding you with dramatic scenery at every step.

The journey begins at Xyloskalo, where sweeping views of towering cliffs set the tone for an unforgettable trek. As you descend into the gorge, the trail winds through shaded pine forests, past ancient cypress trees, and alongside a crystal-clear river that follows you nearly the entire way. The highlight for many hikers is the famous “Iron Gates,” a narrow passage where the canyon walls rise almost 300 meters high and squeeze to just 3 meters apart—one of the most iconic natural landmarks in all of Greece.

Along the way, you’ll encounter the abandoned village of Samaria, once home to a small mountain community, and now a peaceful rest area full of history and charm. The gorge is also a protected habitat for rare wildlife, including the endangered Cretan ibex (kri-kri), often spotted on the rocky slopes.

Vikos Gorge in the Pindus Mountains

Hidden within the dramatic peaks of the Pindus Mountains in northwestern Greece, Vikos Gorge stands as one of the deepest and most impressive canyons in the world. Located in the enchanting region of Zagori, this natural wonder offers a hiking experience defined by untouched wilderness, geological marvels, and serene mountain beauty.

The trail through Vikos Gorge leads you along ancient stone paths, riverbeds, and forested slopes that reveal a landscape shaped over millions of years. Towering limestone walls rise vertically on both sides—reaching heights of up to 1,000 meters—while the crystal-blue waters of the Voidomatis River weave through the valley floor. The scenery shifts constantly, from shaded forests filled with aromatic herbs to wide-open vistas where the gorge stretches into the distance like a natural cathedral.

Highlights of the hike include the Vikos–Megalo Papingo and Monodendri–Vikos routes, each offering spectacular lookout points such as Oxia and Beloi, where the enormity of the gorge becomes truly breathtaking. As you walk, you may spot rare flora and fauna, including endemic plants used for centuries by Zagori’s famed herbal healers.

Mount Olympus

Rising majestically above the northern Greek landscape, Mount Olympus is far more than the country’s highest peak—it is a place where myth and nature intertwine. Known in ancient Greek mythology as the home of Zeus and the twelve Olympian gods, this awe-inspiring mountain continues to captivate visitors with its dramatic ridges, deep forests, and spiritual allure.

A hike through Mount Olympus National Park takes you into a world of striking contrasts. Lush forests of pine, fir, and beech blanket the lower slopes, giving way to alpine meadows and rocky, moon-like ridges as you ascend. The trails lead past rushing streams, wooden bridges, and viewpoints that open onto sweeping vistas of the Aegean Sea and the Thessalian plains.

The renowned route to Mytikas, the highest summit at 2,918 meters, offers an unforgettable challenge. The final ascent involves scrambling over rugged terrain, rewarding you with a breathtaking panorama from the very throne of the gods. For those seeking gentler adventure, routes to Prionia, Skolio, or the mountain’s historic refuges provide rich natural beauty without the technical climbs.
